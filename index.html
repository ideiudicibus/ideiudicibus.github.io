NoSQL Patterns: denormalize your mind 

By Ignazio De Iudicibus posted 22,June 2017

Previous Next Close
In the RDBMS world that is relational databases, the data modeling processes typically starts by modeling data as a series of tables, consisting of rows and columns, which define the so called schema of your data. Relational database theory has a well defined approach to this practice   referred to as design date in a normal forms. 
In short, there are two forms of normal forms that are of particular interest:  first normal form (1NF)  and third  normal form (3NF).
We can  consider 1NF data to be any data that’s tabular that is "made of cells"  that contains exactly one value. This requirement  that each cell contains exactly one value is, as we’ll see later, a requirement that in the NoSQL world (MongoDB)  does not impose, with the potential for some nice performance gains.
